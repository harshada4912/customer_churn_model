1. Overall Churn Rate
- A significant percentage of customers are leaving the service.
- High churn directly reduces revenue and increases acquisition costs.

2. Subscription Type Behavior
- Basic/low-tier subscription plans show higher churn rates.
- Premium users tend to stay longer.

3. Contract Length Impact
- Month-to-month customers churn more frequently.
- Long-term contracts have better retention.

4. Support Calls Effect
- Customers with more support calls are more likely to churn.
- Indicates dissatisfaction or service/product issues.

5. Payment Delay Pattern
- Customers with frequent payment delays show higher churn.
- May reflect low commitment or financial difficulties.

6. Usage Frequency
- Low usage customers are highly likely to churn.
- Engagement level strongly correlates with retention.

7. Tenure Trend
- New customers (first year) churn the most.
- Retention improves with longer tenure.

8. Revenue Loss
- Churned customers contribute to significant revenue loss.
- Losing high-value customers has a larger financial impact.

9. High-Risk Segment
- Customers with high support calls + high delays + low usage show the highest churn probability.
- These customers can be predicted early.

10. Customer Lifetime Value (CLV)
- Long-tenure and high-spend customers generate maximum profit.
- Retaining them is critical for business growth.
